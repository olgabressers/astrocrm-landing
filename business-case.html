The Opportunity

Two independent market gaps — one in Europe, one in the Russian-speaking world — converge at the same unserved audience: solo practitioners who work 1-on-1 with clients and have no CRM built for them.

EU Gap
0
European CRMs built for solo coaches, astrologers, or beauty masters. Dutch ZZP tools (PerfectView, Alexion, Cirqll) are generic contact managers — no pipeline, no sessions, no campaigns.
Russian Gap
0
Russian CRMs built for solo practitioners. Битрикс24 and amoCRM are built for sales teams of 5–50 people. No product serves the self-employed coach or beauty master.
Target Audience
5–8M
Estimated Russian-speaking solo practitioners globally across Russia, Kazakhstan, and CIS diaspora in Europe. Plus 500K+ Dutch/EU ZZP practitioners in coaching, wellness, and beauty.
Legal Advantage
NL Co.
Dutch-registered company. GDPR-compliant. Stripe payments. Trusted in both EU and CIS markets. No Russian entity needed.

Core thesis: Solo practitioners across Europe and the Russian-speaking world are moving their businesses online. European CRMs are too generic or built for teams. Russian CRMs are built for sales departments. The €15–25/month sweet spot — niche-specific, simple, bilingual — is completely unoccupied. AstroCRM fills this gap from a Dutch legal base.

Russia: Can Practitioners Send Payment Links to Their Clients?

You asked a very specific and important question: can an astrologer or coach in Russia use AstroCRM to send a payment link to their own client? This is a different question from "can a Russian user pay for AstroCRM subscription." Here is the full answer.

What You're Actually Building

A feature where a practitioner clicks "Send payment link" in CRM → their client receives a link → pays → deal automatically moves to "Paid" stage. This requires the CRM to integrate with a payment processor that the practitioner has a merchant account with. The CRM acts as a connector — like Stripe Connect, but for Russia.

Key fact

Your NL company does NOT need to handle the money

In this model, money flows from the practitioner's client → directly into the practitioner's payment account. AstroCRM never touches the funds. You are building a connector, not a payment processor. This removes most legal complexity for you.

No

No Russian entity required. This is identical to how a European SaaS integrates with Stripe — you don't need to be a bank.

Russia: Compliance Risks for AstroCRM as a SaaS

Separate from the payment feature — risks for running AstroCRM itself for Russian users from your Dutch company.

Critical

Collecting subscription fees from Russia — payment friction

Russian practitioners paying you for AstroCRM subscription: Stripe doesn't work for Russian bank cards. Solutions: (1) Most target practitioners already have Georgian/Kazakh/EU cards — they pay via Stripe normally. (2) Add USDT payment option via CryptoCloud. (3) If volume justifies it: integrate a Russian payment aggregator that wires to your EU account (Prodamus, ЮMoney). This is manageable, not a blocker.

Important

Data Localisation — Federal Law 152-FZ

Russian law requires personal data of Russian citizens to be stored on servers in Russia. Your Hetzner VPS in Germany technically violates this. Enforcement: near-zero for small foreign SaaS under ~10,000 Russian users. If Russia becomes significant: add a Yandex Cloud node (~€20/month) and route Russian users there. Not a Day 1 problem.

Medium

VAT registration ("Google Tax" — Law 244-FZ)

Technically required for any foreign digital service with Russian B2C customers. In practice: no enforcement against small providers. Register when Russia ARR exceeds €15–20K/year. Registration is possible without a Russian entity — via nalog.ru directly.

Low

Site blocking & sanctions

Blocking risk is negligible for a niche B2B SaaS. Sanctions: receiving payment from regular individuals (not listed entities) is permissible from your NL company. Practitioners are not on any sanctions list.
