Why You Need a CRM

Most solo practitioners lose 30–50% of potential revenue — without realising it

A CRM (Client Relationship Manager) is simply a tool that remembers every client, every conversation, and every follow-up — so you don't have to. Here's what changes when you use one:

You're losing leads you forgot about

Someone messaged you 3 weeks ago about a consultation. You meant to reply. You forgot. That's €150–500 gone. A CRM never forgets — every new inquiry is captured and tracked until it becomes a client or a clear "no".

Result: 20–40% more conversions

Returning clients are your biggest revenue source

A client who came for a natal chart 6 months ago is your warmest lead for a solar return reading. Without a CRM you have no systematic way to reach them. With one, a single click sends a personalised follow-up to 50 past clients.

Result: 2–3× more repeat bookings

Your client history is scattered everywhere

Session notes in a notebook. Files in Google Drive. Messages in Telegram. Payment confirmations in email. A CRM puts everything — notes, files, charts, history — in one place for each client. You look professional and remember everything.

Result: 1–2 hrs saved per day

You can't grow what you can't measure

How many new leads did you get this month? What's your conversion rate? Which service makes the most money? Without a CRM you're flying blind. With one, your reports answer these questions in 10 seconds — so you know where to focus.

Result: Smarter business decisions

Admin is eating your billable hours

Every hour you spend chasing payments, copying client details between apps, or manually writing follow-up messages is an hour not spent with a paying client. CRM automates the repetitive parts so you do them once, not every week.

Result: 5–8 hrs/week back

Clients feel the difference immediately

When you remember a client's daughter's name, the topic from last session, and send a follow-up exactly when they need it — they tell their friends. A CRM makes every client feel like your only client, even when you have 100.

Result: More referrals & reviews

What makes AstroCRM unique

What are the 2 business workspaces?

Most practitioners run two fundamentally different types of work — and they need different pipelines. AstroCRM keeps them separate in one account.

Workspace 1 — people & relationship model
The cycle never ends at one sale

Your client pays, but the relationship doesn't close — it deepens. They come back for the next session, the next reading, the next program. You track the whole journey: consultation history, notes, follow-ups, life events. The relationship itself is the product.

Examples: astrology & ba-zi consultations, coaching programs, psychotherapy, energy healing, human design sessions, numerology, feng shui projects

Workspace 2 — standard product or service sell
Classic lead → close → next

Client enquires, gets a quote, pays, done. The deal closes and you move on. You still track buyers and follow up — but each sale is its own event, not part of a long-term journey. A standard pipeline: lead in, money out, repeat.

Examples: online courses, astrology reports, product packages, one-off workshops, digital downloads, merchandise, beauty or styling services with a fixed menu

Both workspaces are fully independent — separate pipelines, contacts, and automations — inside one subscription.
