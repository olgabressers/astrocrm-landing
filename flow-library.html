2 · Universal patterns — build once, into every template

Patterns that repeat across all 20 products studied. These belong to the platform layer, not to any single template.

The universal skeleton every vertical follows
Lead phase→Booked = contract / first payment→Delivery phase→Rebook / Renewal / Win-back
  • Two-phase pipeline: lead stages, then delivery stages. HoneyBook (Inquiry → Follow Up → Proposal Sent → booked stages), Dubsado (separate "Lead statuses" and "Job statuses"), SimplePractice (Prospective → Active), Clio Grow (Intake / Hired / Not hired). "Booked" is defined by contract signed and/or first payment everywhere.
  • 24 hours before is the universal reminder anchor. Fresha default 24h (range 2–72h, optional second at 1h); GlossGenius reminder 24h + confirmation request 72h; Booksy 24h free text; Delenta factory defaults 24h + 1h + 15min; Paperbell's template suggests "1 day before"; TherapyNotes emails in a 60–24h window.
  • Rebooking / win-back nudges are default or near-default. GlossGenius: 6 weeks after last appointment (customizable 1–12, suppressed if client rebooks); Booksy: 3 weeks after last visit; Fresha "Reminder to rebook" on by default; Walla win-back on day 5 and day 20 after plan expiry; CoachAccountable "Schedule Nudge" after N days with nothing upcoming.
  • Package = N sessions with a visible "sessions remaining" counter. Paperbell, CoachAccountable (allocation + "3 appointments left" notices), Delenta ("Outstanding Sessions"), Healthie (1 credit = 1 session). Memberships/subscriptions are separate: recurring billing, freeze/pause, automatic payment retries (Momence: 3 retries then inactive; Trainerize dunning at failure, +3d, +5d).
  • Minimum intake = name + email. Dubsado requires first/last/email; HoneyBook's non-deletable fields are Full Name + Email; My Music Staff requires only first + last name. Intake forms attach to trigger events (booking, purchase, first session) — never sent manually.
  • No-show economics, one shape everywhere: deposit at booking OR card-on-file cancellation fee + business-defined window. No vendor publishes a default fee %.
  • Client-chase reminders: TaxDome default for any pending item = 1 reminder, 3 days after sending. 17hats invoices: 1 day before due + monthly past-due. HoneyBook ships all automatic payment reminders ON.
  • Stage moves are event-driven: contract signed / payment made / form submitted advance the pipeline automatically (HoneyBook, Dubsado, Clio Grow, TaxDome).
  • Vocabulary: "client" is near-universal on the business side. Client-facing language says "session"; operational language says "appointment". Tutoring says "student" + "family"; legal "matter"; tax "job".
  • Nobody deletes clients with history — archive/deactivate preserves records (TherapyNotes even auto-inactivates 4 months after the last appointment).

5 · Coach

Loads for: life coaches · business coaches · executive coaches · career coaches
AstroCRM default flow
Inquiry→Discovery Call→Package Proposed→Package Purchased→Coaching Active (X of Y)→Completed / Renewal
Sources: Paperbell · CoachAccountable · Delenta help docs.
Paperbell's documented client journey
Package page→Schedule session(s)→Sign contract→Pay→Complete survey→Client portal
  • Paperbell ships two pre-built packages: "Free Discovery" (1 × 30-min session) and "$100 Breakthrough" (1 × 60-min session). Free discovery calls are a free package, so leads enter the same system.
  • Package structure: one-time payment, monthly subscription (N sessions/month), or payment plan. CoachAccountable "Engagements" track an allocation (e.g. 12 sessions / 10 hours) with a progress bar; Delenta shows "Outstanding Sessions" on the client card.
  • Reminders: Delenta factory defaults = 24h, 1h and 15min before each session. Paperbell by default sends only a purchase receipt — everything else opt-in (template suggests "1 day before"; SMS 1 day / 1 hour).
  • Book-your-next-session nudges: Paperbell "Reminder to Book" (fires when unbooked sessions remain); CoachAccountable "Schedule Nudge" (N days since last appointment) and allocation notices ("you have 3 appointments left").
  • Completion touchpoint: Paperbell's "Final Appointment" email template explicitly asks for a testimonial and referrals.
  • Intake: no vendor ships default questions — forms attach to triggers (Purchase / Appointment / Final Appointment). Default question set: NOT PUBLIC anywhere — ours to define.

SettingDefault value
Intake fieldsGoal for coaching · current situation · how they found you
Automations ONPurchase receipt · session reminder 24h · reminder-to-book when sessions remain unbooked · "sessions remaining" notice near end of package · final-session testimonial/renewal message
PackagesON, front and centre — incl. a pre-built free Discovery package (Paperbell precedent)

8 · Studio / Class

Loads for: yoga instructors · massage therapists (class- and pass-based practices)
AstroCRM default flow
Booked→Waitlist (if full)→Checked-in→Completed→Pass Renewal / Win-back
Sources: Mindbody · Momence · Walla docs.
Documented class lifecycle (all three vendors)
Booked→Waitlisted (auto-promote via SMS)→Checked-in→Early / Late cancel / No-show
  • No-show is time-derived, not manual: Mindbody auto-marks "absent" at class start; Momence gives 24h after class end to check in; Walla delays fee collection 12–24h so staff can reverse charges.
  • Waitlist auto-promotion with SMS is the default: Mindbody "Auto-add" / "First to Claim"; Momence first-come / double-opt-in (10 min to reply) / fast-fingers; Walla auto-add with position visible.

  • Passes: class credits with expiry (Momence: 6 months / 1 year / infinite; "Activate on First Use" delays the clock), optional auto-refill; subscriptions with rollover credits, freezes, 3 payment retries before deactivation.
  • Waivers are business-configured, signed at booking or check-in, stored on the profile with timestamp. Class-reminder default hours: NOT PUBLIC in all three — ours to set.

Method & source notes

  • Research ran on 15 July 2026 across the official public help centers of: Fresha, GlossGenius, Booksy, Mindbody, Momence, Walla, SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Practice Better, Trainerize, Healthie, Paperbell, CoachAccountable, Delenta, My Music Staff, TutorBird, Teachworks, HoneyBook, Dubsado, 17hats, Wealthbox, Clio, TaxDome — plus a dedicated market scan for spiritual-practitioner tools.
  • Several help centers (SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Clio, Wealthbox, Booksy) block direct page fetches; their articles were retrieved via the same vendors' public Zendesk APIs — content identical to the live pages cited.
  • Flow structures (stage names, field lists, cadences) are functional ideas and not copyrightable; no vendor text, UI or branding is reproduced in the product.
  • Where a vendor does not document a default publicly, it is marked NOT PUBLIC — those values are AstroCRM design decisions, never presented as sourced facts.
