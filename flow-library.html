2 · Universal patterns — build these once, into every template

Patterns that repeat across all 20 products studied. These belong to the platform layer, not to any single template.

  • Two-phase pipeline: lead stages, then delivery stages. HoneyBook (Inquiry → Follow Up → Proposal Sent → then booked stages), Dubsado (separate "Lead statuses" and "Job statuses" sections), SimplePractice (Prospective → Active), Clio Grow (fixed buckets: Intake / Hired / Not hired). "Booked" is defined by contract signed and/or first payment everywhere.
  • 24 hours before is the universal reminder anchor. Fresha default reminder 24h (range 2–72h, optional second at 1h); GlossGenius reminder 24h + separate confirmation request 72h before; Booksy 24h free text; Delenta factory defaults 24h + 1h + 15min; Paperbell's suggested template "1 day before"; TherapyNotes sends email reminders in a 60–24h window.
  • Rebooking / win-back nudges are default or near-default. GlossGenius: 6 weeks after last appointment by default (customizable 1–12 weeks, suppressed if the client rebooks); Booksy template: 3 weeks after last visit; Fresha "Reminder to rebook" is on by default; Walla win-back fires day 5 and day 20 after a plan expires; CoachAccountable "Schedule Nudge" fires after N days with no upcoming appointment.
  • Package = N sessions with a visible "sessions remaining" counter. Paperbell (remaining bookable appointments), CoachAccountable (allocation + progress bar + "you have 3 appointments left" notices), Delenta ("Outstanding Sessions"), Healthie (1 credit = 1 session). Memberships/subscriptions are a separate recurring construct with freeze/pause and automatic payment-retry (Momence: 3 retries then inactive; Trainerize: fixed dunning messages at failure, +3 days, +5 days).
  • Minimum intake = name + email. Dubsado requires first name, last name, email; HoneyBook's two non-deletable fields are Full Name and Email; My Music Staff requires only first + last name. Everything else is optional/custom. Intake forms attach to trigger events (booking made, package purchased, first session) — not sent manually.
  • No-show economics, one shape everywhere: deposit at booking OR card-on-file cancellation fee, a business-defined cancellation window, fee charged when marked no-show. No vendor publishes a default fee % — the mechanism is standard, the number is the practitioner's choice.
  • Client-chase reminders: TaxDome's global default for any pending item (form, e-signature, invoice) is 1 reminder, 3 days after sending. 17hats invoices: 1 day before due + monthly when past due. HoneyBook ships all automatic payment reminders ON by default.
  • Stage moves are event-driven, not manual: contract signed / payment made / form submitted advance the pipeline automatically (HoneyBook, Dubsado, Clio Grow workflows, TaxDome automations).
  • Vocabulary: "client" is near-universal on the business side (never "customer", except Momence). Client-facing language says "session"; operational/calendar language says "appointment". Tutoring says "student" + "family"; legal says "matter"; tax says "job".
  • Nobody deletes clients with history — deactivation/archiving preserves records everywhere (My Music Staff, TherapyNotes auto-inactive after 4 months since last appointment, SimplePractice Archive).

5 · Template 3 — Coach

Loads for: life coaches · business coaches · executive coaches · career coaches
Sources: Paperbell · CoachAccountable · Delenta help docs.

Default client journey (sourced)

Discovery (free package) → Package purchase → Contract signed → Payment → Intake survey → Sessions from allocation → Final session
  • Paperbell's documented 8-step client flow: package page → review → schedule session(s) → sign contract → pay → complete survey → notification emails → client portal. New accounts ship with two pre-built packages: "Free Discovery" (1 × 30-min session) and "$100 Breakthrough" (1 × 60-min session). Free discovery calls are handled as a free package so leads enter the same system.
  • Package structure: one-time payment, monthly subscription (N sessions/month), or payment plan. CoachAccountable "Engagements" track an allocation (e.g. 12 sessions or 10 hours) with a progress bar; Delenta shows "Outstanding Sessions" on the client card.
  • Reminders: Delenta factory defaults = 24h, 1h and 15min before each session. Paperbell by default sends only a purchase receipt — everything else is opt-in (its template suggests a reminder "1 day before"; SMS options 1 day / 1 hour).
  • Book-your-next-session nudges: Paperbell "Reminder to Book" (fires when unbooked sessions remain in a package); CoachAccountable "Schedule Nudge" (N days since last appointment with nothing upcoming) and allocation notices ("you have 3 appointments left").
  • Completion touchpoint: Paperbell's "Final Appointment" email template explicitly asks for a testimonial and referrals.
  • Intake: no vendor ships default questions — forms attach to triggers (Purchase / Appointment / Final Appointment in Paperbell; intake worksheet inside a CoachAccountable Offering). Default question set: NOT PUBLIC anywhere — ours to define.

6 · Template 4 — Therapist

Loads for: psychologists · psychotherapists · CBT therapists · family therapists
Sources: SimplePractice · TherapyNotes help docs.

Default lifecycle (sourced)

Inquiry (Prospective) → Intake documents → Active, recurring weekly sessions → Inactive / Terminated
  • SimplePractice client statuses (exact): Prospective · Active · Inactive · Archived · Waitlist. New contact-form inquiries auto-create a Prospective client on an "Inquiries" page with an intake checklist (prescreener → intake documents → appointment). Conversion to Active is manual.
  • TherapyNotes auto-transitions: new clients with no appointments become inactive after 1 month; active clients auto-inactive 4 months after their last appointment, or on signing a Termination Note.
  • Default intake packet ships in the box. TherapyNotes includes by default: Client Information Form, Client History Form, Consent for Services, Client Insurance Form, Release of Information. SimplePractice pre-loads consent templates by specialty (Informed Consent for Psychotherapy, Privacy Practices, Practice Policies…) plus built-in Demographics and Credit-card forms; intake package auto-sends with the Welcome email when a client is added.
  • Reminders: SimplePractice offers 24/36/48/72h lead times (email/text/voice, per-client consent). TherapyNotes: email sent 60–24h before; SMS 28–2h before ($0.14); queued 1h after the appointment is created.
  • Cancellation policy options (SimplePractice, exact): phone-call-only / 24h / 48h / 72h; cancelling inside the window → status "Late Canceled", invoiced by default.
  • Notes chain (both vendors): Intake Note → Treatment Plan → per-session Progress Notes (SOAP / DAP / BIRP templates) → Termination/Discharge note. Notes are generated from the scheduled appointment.

7 · Template 5 — Nutrition / Training

Loads for: nutritionists · dietitians · personal trainers
Sources: Practice Better · Trainerize · Healthie help docs.
Prospect → Invitation / activation → Intake flow → Consult → Program with check-ins → Renewal
  • Lead state: Practice Better auto-creates a "Prospective Client" from any public form or booking request (they receive no automated messages until converted via "Review & Add"); Trainerize has a "Prospect" client type with its own booking link.
  • Intake ships on by default: Trainerize's E-Consultation form is "turned on by default" plus a generic PARQ PDF in the welcome email. Healthie auto-sends a Default Intake Flow when a client is added, and ships a large named form library out of the box (New Patient Assessment, Health History, cancellation policies, liability waiver, PHQ-9/GAD-7, Pre-Coaching Questionnaire…).

  • Check-in cadence: Trainerize schedules progress photos + weigh-ins "at the start and end of each training phase" by default; programs are built in 7-day weeks (example in docs: 4 phases × 4 weeks).
  • Reminders: Healthie offers 1h / 2h / 1–4 days before (email + SMS); Practice Better up to 3 reminders per session.
  • Programs: drip content by calendar date, elapsed time, or module completion (Practice Better fixed-date / evergreen / self-paced); packages: 1 credit = 1 session (Healthie).

8 · Template 6 — Studio / Class

Loads for: yoga instructors · massage therapists (class- and pass-based practices)
Sources: Mindbody · Momence · Walla docs.
Booked → Waitlisted (auto-promote) → Checked-in → or Early cancel / Late cancel / No-show
  • No-show is time-derived, not manual: Mindbody auto-marks "absent" at class start; Momence gives 24h after class end to check in before no-show; Walla delays fee collection 12–24h so staff can reverse charges.
  • Waitlist auto-promotion with SMS is the default pattern: Mindbody "Auto-add" / "First to Claim"; Momence first-come-first-serve / double-opt-in (10 min to reply) / fast-fingers; Walla auto-add with position visible to the client.
  • Win-back defaults (Walla, exact): contact clients on day 5 and day 20 after their plan expires. Momence ships 8 pre-built "Sequences" incl. Winback, Customer Birthday, Milestones (25th/50th/75th visit), Intro Offer Funnel, Abandoned Checkout. Mindbody has a "Visits Remaining Low" auto email.
  • Passes: class credits with expiry (Momence: 6 months / 1 year / infinite; "Activate on First Use" delays the clock until first use), optional auto-refill; subscriptions with usage limits, rollover credits, freezes and 3 payment retries before deactivation.
  • Waivers are business-configured (not preloaded), signed at booking or check-in, stored on the profile with timestamp.
  • Class-reminder default hours: NOT PUBLIC in all three — ours to set.

9 · Template 7 — Beauty Appointment

Loads for: lash & brow artists · nail artists · tattoo artists · hair stylists
Sources: Fresha · GlossGenius · Booksy help docs.

Fresha's exact default appointment statuses

Booked → Confirmed → Completed (at checkout) · Cancelled · No-show (+ custom, e.g. "Arrived", "Started")
  • Reminder defaults: GlossGenius sends a confirmation request 72h before + reminder 24h before (all notifications on by default at account creation); Fresha ships two reminders by default, first at 24h (range 2–72h, optional second 1h before); Booksy sends a free 24h text.
  • Rebooking nudge: GlossGenius default = 6 weeks after the last appointment (customizable 1–12 weeks, text-only, offered at checkout, suppressed if the client rebooks); Booksy template = 3 weeks after last visit; Fresha's "Reminder to rebook" is on by default with a per-service timescale.
  • Post-visit: Fresha "Thank you for visiting" sends at checkout with review prompt, tip and invoice; GlossGenius sends a thank-you email with the booking URL. Birthday and win-back-lapsed-clients messages exist as opt-in automations with attachable discount deals (Fresha).
  • No-show protection: deposits at booking OR card-on-file cancellation fee within a set window (all three). Booksy auto-charges no-show fees at end of business day. Fresha has a dedicated patch-test record (Pending/Passed/Failed, valid 6 months, checked at online booking).
  • Intake: service-linked forms sent automatically at booking, completed before arrival; Booksy's own example question: "Do you have any allergies?"; frequency controls = once / once a year / every appointment (GlossGenius). Booksy seeds the service menu automatically from the business category chosen at signup.
  • Packages/memberships: package = prepaid N sessions with optional expiry (GlossGenius example: "5 facials over 6 months"); membership = recurring credits/discounts.

10 · Template 8 — Expert Consultant

Loads for: financial advisors · tax & accounting consultants · legal advisors
Sources: Wealthbox · Clio Grow · TaxDome help docs.

Documented default stages

  • Wealthbox (exact factory defaults): Evaluation → Identify Decision Makers → Qualification → Needs Analysis → Review → Proposal — Won/Lost are terminal statuses hidden from the board.
  • Clio Grow: three fixed buckets — Intake / Hired / Not hired — with editable starter statuses; Clio's recommended example: First contact → Schedule Appointment → Send Intake Form → Follow Up → Send Fee Agreement.
  • TaxDome (documented 1040 pipeline example): Review Docs → Prepare Engagement Letter → Prepare Return → Review; marketplace templates for Tax Return, Bookkeeping, Payroll ship with automations included.
  • The engagement letter sits at the front of delivery in all three — between "agreed to work together" and "doing the work".
  • Client-chase default (TaxDome, exact): every pending item (organizer/questionnaire, e-signature, invoice, proposal) gets 1 automatic reminder, 3 days after sending. Clio supports up to 5 appointment reminders per appointment (email + text).
  • Recurring cycle is job-level: TaxDome re-spawns recurring jobs (annually, quarterly, monthly, custom) into the same pipeline; Wealthbox handles annual reviews via recurring tasks + "Last Review Date" custom fields; Wealthbox ships a library of 10 pre-built workflow templates (lead-gen, onboarding, client review…).
  • Data collection is a pipeline step: Clio intake forms auto-create a matter in a chosen status; TaxDome organizers auto-send on stage entry and auto-advance the job on submission.

11 · Template 9 — Tutor / Teacher

Loads for: music teachers · language tutors
Sources: My Music Staff · TutorBird · Teachworks docs.

Default student statuses (My Music Staff / TutorBird, exact)

Lead → Waiting → Trial → Active → Inactive
  • Lead and Waiting students cannot be scheduled but can be emailed; Trial behaves exactly like Active (a segmentation label); Inactive preserves attendance, notes and financial history and revokes portal access. Teachworks collapses pre-enrollment into one "Prospective" status.
  • The payer is a separate entity — the "Family" account. Students are Child (billed via parent) or Adult/Independent. Only first + last name are required to create a student. Family status auto-follows student status.
  • Billing modes (exact): per lesson · per month · hourly (My Music Staff/TutorBird); Teachworks adds prepaid Package and Flat Fee. Both MMS and TutorBird seed a default rate of $30 per 30-minute lesson. Auto-invoicing generates and emails invoices on a schedule, with a heads-up summary to the teacher a few days before.
  • Make-up credit engine (MMS/TutorBird): the attendance dropdown simultaneously records presence, decides billing, and issues/consumes duration-based make-up credits (statuses incl. "Absent, Issue Make-Up", "Present, Use Make-Up Credit", "Absent, Notice Given"); credits are per-teacher with an optional cap.
  • Trial lessons: bookable from the sign-up form; free trial = price override to $0 (TutorBird).
  • Reminders: hours-before value set per event category, opt-in per student (email + SMS); overdue-invoice reminder is a checkbox with user-set days-after-due. Vendor default hours: NOT PUBLIC.

12 · Template 10 — Project Creative

Loads for: wedding planners · interior designers (project-based, milestone-billed work)
Sources: HoneyBook · Dubsado · 17hats help docs.

Documented default stages

  • HoneyBook (named in official docs): Inquiry → Follow Up → Proposal Sent → Proposal Signed → Retainer Paid → Proposal Paid New leads land in "Inquiry" automatically.
  • Dubsado's documented example set: Lead statuses — Inquiry, Consultation Booked, Proposal Sent, Contract Pending; Job statuses — Active Project, Revisions, Final Delivery, Complete.
