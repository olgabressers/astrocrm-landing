Workflow — From Zero to 3-Language Video

1
Claude writes the script — word for word, 60–90 seconds. Also generates 3–5 variations for A/B testing. SEO title, description, tags for YouTube included.
2
Paste script into HeyGen — choose an AI avatar (professional female, Russian-speaking), choose background (office, abstract, branded). Render takes ~2 minutes.
3
Use HeyGen Video Translation — select English and Dutch. HeyGen auto-dubs and lip-syncs the avatar into both languages. One click → 3 videos.
4
For Reels/Shorts — use Invideo AI instead. One-sentence prompt: "60-second Instagram Reel for AstroCRM, CRM for astrology coaches, Russian language, mystical but professional tone." It writes + builds the full video.
5
Post: YouTube (Russian + EN + NL versions) · Instagram Reels (Russian primary) · Telegram (link to YouTube). Total time per video set: ~30 minutes once workflow is set up.

Skills required — and can your teenagers do it?

Skills needed
  • Copy/paste text into a web form
  • Navigate English-language web interface
  • Choose an avatar from a gallery (like picking a profile photo)
  • Click render / download buttons
  • Upload a video file to YouTube or Instagram
  • Write a short post caption (Claude helps with this)

No video editing. No recording. No design skills. No technical knowledge.

Can teenagers run this?

Yes — completely. HeyGen and Invideo are designed for non-technical users. Any teenager comfortable with Instagram or TikTok will pick this up in one session.

How to split the work:

  • You: give a 1-line brief ("video about X for astrologers")
  • Claude: writes the full script + post caption
  • Teenager: pastes into HeyGen/Invideo, clicks render, downloads, posts

Realistic: 1–2 training sessions with your kid → they own the whole production pipeline.

  • What topic each video covers
  • Whether the final result fits your brand

Strategic decisions stay with you. Execution can be fully delegated — to a teenager, a VA, or just run yourself in under an hour/week.

Where to Invest First to Sign More Customers

Tutorials vs avatar promotions — the honest answer for AstroCRM at this stage
Step 1 — Do This First
Tutorials with Guidde
Free · 5–10 min per video · starts converting immediately
  • Your audience has never used CRM. Their #1 objection: "Is it complicated?"
  • A 2-min tutorial on your landing page that shows it takes 3 clicks to add a client kills that objection instantly
  • Embed tutorials on astrocrm.app → waitlist visitors see the product before signing up → higher conversion
  • Free plan covers 25 tutorials — zero cost until you have paying clients
  • Also doubles as onboarding material: new clients watch tutorials → fewer support questions

ROI: direct. Someone sees the tutorial → signs up. No audience building needed.

Step 2 — Do This Once You Have Budget
Avatar Promotions (HeyGen + Invideo)
$54/mo · awareness + reach · audience takes time to build
  • Great for finding new people who don't know AstroCRM exists yet
  • YouTube and Instagram Reels are top-of-funnel — they create awareness, not direct sign-ups
  • Takes 2–3 months of consistent posting before the algorithm starts distributing your content
  • Much higher impact once you have 5–10 testimonials from real clients to reference in the script
  • Without social proof, cold-audience videos convert poorly

ROI: indirect and delayed. Important for scale, but not urgent at zero-client stage.

Bottom line: Make 5 Guidde tutorials now (free, takes 1 hour total) and embed them on the landing page. This will convert more waitlist visitors than any YouTube channel you could build in the next 6 months. Start avatar promotions once you have your first 10 paying clients and something real to show — then the videos will say "used by 10+ practitioners" and that changes everything.

Why Option 1 (HeyGen + Invideo) and not just one tool?

HeyGen avatar video = looks like a real spokesperson. Builds trust. Works for YouTube (people watch 2–3 minutes). Converts well for "what is AstroCRM" content. Auto-dubs into Russian + English + Dutch — 3 videos from 1 recording. But avatar videos feel slower and more formal — not ideal for Instagram Reels.

Invideo AI stock footage = fast, punchy, native to Reels/Shorts. "60-second problem/solution" format. Feels like a real ad. Works better for cold audiences who scroll quickly. But no avatar = less personal, harder to build brand recognition over time.

Combined: HeyGen for YouTube (long-form trust), Invideo for Reels (short-form reach). Different jobs, different formats, both fully automated. That's why both.
